# Content Pillars

Five strategic content pillars that guide topic selection for the Lensmor blog. Each pillar represents a distinct value area for our audience.

These pillars are a starting point — adjust as the business evolves and new audience needs emerge.

---

## 1. Event Intelligence & Preparation

**Focus**: How to research, prepare for, and choose the right events.

**Target Personas**:
- Event marketers planning their annual event calendar
- First-time exhibitors unsure where to start
- Marketing managers evaluating which trade shows deserve budget

**Funnel Stages**: Awareness, Consideration

**Topic Directions**:
1. How to research and evaluate trade shows before committing
2. Pre-event planning checklists and timelines
3. How to set measurable goals for event participation
4. Choosing between different event formats (trade shows, conferences, summits)
5. Regional vs. national vs. international event strategy
6. Pre-event outreach and appointment setting
7. Budgeting and resource allocation for events

**Lensmor Product Tie-in**: Lensmor's event intelligence features help teams research exhibitors, attendees, and event history before committing resources. Pre-event data reduces wasted spend.

---

## 2. Trade Show ROI & Measurement

**Focus**: Proving and improving the return on event investments.

**Target Personas**:
- Marketing directors justifying event budgets to leadership
- Event managers tracking performance across multiple shows
- CFOs/finance teams evaluating marketing channel ROI

**Funnel Stages**: Consideration, Decision

**Topic Directions**:
1. How to calculate true event ROI (beyond lead count)
2. Benchmarking event performance across shows
3. Attribution models for event-sourced revenue
4. Cost-per-lead at events vs. other channels
5. Post-event reporting templates and frameworks
6. When to cut an underperforming event from the calendar

**Lensmor Product Tie-in**: Lensmor tracks engagement metrics per event, enabling accurate ROI calculations and cross-event benchmarking. Data replaces gut feeling in budget decisions.

**Target Personas**:
- Sales teams working event leads
- Event marketers responsible for lead handoff to sales

**Funnel Stages**: Consideration, Decision

**Topic Directions**:
1. Modern lead capture methods (beyond badge scanning)
2. Lead qualification frameworks for event contacts
3. Speed-to-follow-up: post-event outreach best practices
4. CRM integration and lead routing from events
5. Scoring and prioritizing event leads
6. Nurture sequences for different event lead segments
7. Booth engagement tactics that generate quality conversations
8. Converting event leads vs. inbound leads — what's different

**Lensmor Product Tie-in**: Lensmor captures and enriches lead data in real time during events, with automatic CRM sync. This eliminates manual data entry and accelerates follow-up.

## How to Use This File

When running the content strategy process:
1. **Gap analysis**: Check which pillars have the fewest published articles in the content calendar
2. **Topic brainstorming**: Use the "Topic Directions" lists as starting points, not limits
3. **Audience alignment**: Match the topic to the right persona and funnel stage
4. **Product relevance**: Use the tie-in naturally — not every article needs a product mention, but awareness of the connection helps with CTAs and internal linking
