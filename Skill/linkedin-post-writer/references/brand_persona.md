# Brand Persona & Writing Style Guide

This document defines the personal brand, writing style, and content strategy for LinkedIn posts in English.

## Personal Brand Identity

### Core Positioning
- **Industry Focus**: AI/New Technology, Entrepreneurship/Business, Product/Design
- **Tone**: Approachable, friendly, and relatable while maintaining professionalism
- **Voice**: Conversational yet insightful, using everyday language mixed with professional expertise
- **Perspective**: Share personal experiences and practical insights rather than purely theoretical knowledge

### Target Audience
- Technology professionals and entrepreneurs
- Product managers and designers
- AI enthusiasts and practitioners
- Business leaders interested in innovation

## Writing Style Guidelines

### Tone & Voice Characteristics

**Approachable & Friendly Style**
- Use conversational language as if talking to a colleague over coffee
- Share personal stories and experiences to build connection
- Be authentic and genuine, showing both successes and learnings
- Use humor appropriately to make content engaging
- Avoid overly formal or academic language
- Address readers directly using "you" or "we"
- Write like a human, not a corporate press release

### Content Structure

**Preferred Post Length**: 300-800 characters (Medium posts)

**Effective Opening Strategies**:
1. Start with a relatable scenario or question
2. Share a surprising insight or personal realization
3. Reference a recent experience or conversation
4. Present a common challenge your audience faces
5. Use contrast or contradiction to grab attention

**Middle Content**:
- Develop 2-3 main points with supporting details
- Use personal anecdotes or examples
- Include concrete takeaways or actionable insights
- Balance storytelling with practical value
- Break up text with line breaks for readability

**Closing Techniques**:
1. End with a thought-provoking question
2. Invite engagement and discussion
3. Summarize key takeaway in one sentence
4. Call for readers to share their experiences
5. Leave them with something to think about

### Language Patterns

**Preferred Expressions**:
- "I recently discovered..."
- "Here's what surprised me..."
- "In a conversation with..."
- "This made me realize..."
- "Here's the thing..."
- "What I've learned is..."
- "Something interesting happened..."
- "Let me share a quick observation..."

**Power Words for Engagement**:
- "Surprising", "Unexpected", "Counterintuitive"
- "Simple", "Practical", "Actionable"
- "Real", "Honest", "Raw"
- "Game-changing", "Eye-opening", "Breakthrough"

**Avoid**:
- Overly technical jargon without explanation
- Purely promotional content
- Clickbait or sensational language
- Negative or critical tone without constructive insight
- Corporate buzzwords (synergy, leverage, paradigm shift)
- Lengthy theoretical discussions without practical application
- Starting with "I hope this post finds you well" or similar clich√©s

## Image Guidelines

### Two Image Styles

Every post generates two image styles for the user to choose from:

#### Style 1: Linear Dark Mode
Best for tech/AI/abstract concepts, frameworks, and formulas.
- **Background**: Deep charcoal (#1a1a1a to #0a0a0a)
- **Accent color**: Violet-blue #6B75FF for highlights and connections
- **Elements**: Floating geometric shapes, data streams, glowing connection points
- **Style**: Minimalist, matte finish, abstract high-tech aesthetic
- **Labels**: Simple short labels only (2-5 words max)
- **Constraints**: NO UI chrome, NO dashboards, NO browser frames

#### Style 2: Photo Infographic (Apple Minimalism)
Best for personal branding, opinionated content, and human-centered topics.
- **Background**: Real-life setting (office, home workspace) with natural light
- **Person**: User's photo as character reference - can change expression/pose
- **Labels**: SF Pro style clean sans-serif, generous spacing, one accent color max
- **Colors**: White text, OR one accent (blue #0071E3, orange #FF6B35, green #00D68F)
- **Layout**: Generous negative space, precise alignment, minimal elements
- **Style**: Apple "Behind the Mac" aesthetic - premium, intentional, confident

### General Visual Principles
- Clean, modern, and professional aesthetic
- High quality and crisp imagery (2K, 16:9)
- Consistent visual language across posts
- Minimal text overlay
- Eye-catching but not distracting from the message
- Universal symbols that transcend language barriers

### Image Prompts Enhancement
When generating images, consider:
- The core message or metaphor of the post
- Visual elements that represent the main concept
- Which style (Linear Dark Mode vs Photo Infographic) best fits the content type
- Suitable for business social media context

## Writing Tips

### Hook Writing Framework

**Pattern 1: Surprise/Contrast**
- "Everyone says X. But I've found Y."
- "The opposite of what you'd expect happened."
- "Here's what nobody tells you about..."

**Pattern 2: Specific Moment**
- "Last Tuesday, something clicked for me..."
- "A conversation yesterday changed how I think about..."
- "I made a mistake that taught me..."

**Pattern 3: Observation**
- "I've noticed a pattern across..."
- "3 out of 5 companies I talked to..."
- "Something strange is happening in..."

**Pattern 4: Provocative Question**
- "What if we've been thinking about this wrong?"
- "Should we really be doing X?"
- "When did we all agree that...?"

### Closing Framework

**Pattern 1: Open-Ended Question**
- "What's your experience been?"
- "How do you approach this?"
- "Am I missing something here?"

**Pattern 2: Call to Share**
- "Drop your thoughts in the comments."
- "I'd love to hear your take on this."
- "What would you add to this list?"

**Pattern 3: Reflective Statement**
- "Something worth thinking about."
- "No easy answers, but worth exploring."
- "We're all figuring this out together."

## Grammar & Style Notes

- Use contractions (don't, can't, it's) for conversational tone
- Sentence fragments are OK when used intentionally for impact
- Start sentences with "And" or "But" when it flows naturally
- Use em dashes (‚Äî) or parentheses for asides
- Keep one idea per sentence when possible
- Read your post aloud‚Äîif it sounds stiff, rewrite it
- First person (I, we) is encouraged for authenticity
- Active voice over passive voice

## Cultural Considerations

Since these are English posts for an international audience:
- Avoid idioms or cultural references that don't translate globally
- Use examples that resonate across cultures
- When using specific numbers or data, consider global context
- Keep humor universal and professional
- Be mindful of different business practices across regions
- Use inclusive language
